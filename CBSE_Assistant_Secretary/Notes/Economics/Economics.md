# 📊 Economy & Budget: **General Awareness (Prelims)**

---

## 🧠 1️⃣ Indian Economy Basics

### 📌 What is **GDP**?

- **GDP (Gross Domestic Product)** = Total value of goods & services produced in a year.
- It shows how fast the economy is growing.
  👉 Higher GDP growth = faster economic progress.
  🔹 India’s economy is one of the fastest-growing major economies.

---

### 📌 What is **Inflation**?

- **Inflation** = Rise in prices of goods & services over time.
- Expressed as a **percentage**.
- Controlled by **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** through interest rates & money supply.

---

### 📌 What is **Banking System**?

- RBI = India’s **central bank**
  👉 Controls monetary policy, interest rates, money supply.
- Commercial banks = Provide loans, accept deposits, offer services.

---

## 💰 2️⃣ Union Budget: What It Is

- Annual financial plan of the Government of India.
- Presented by **Finance Minister** in Parliament.
- Shows **expected revenue & expenditure** for next financial year (Apr–Mar).
  👉 Includes tax proposals, spending on sectors, welfare schemes, infrastructure, etc.

---

## 📌 3️⃣ Recent Budget Highlights (2024–25 & 2025–26)

### 🟡 **Budget Expenditure & Receipts**

- Govt plans big spending on growth but also tries to reduce deficits.
- Fiscal deficit (gap between revenue & expenditure) is a key figure
  ➤ Around **4.9% of GDP** in Budget 2024–25 estimates.

📌 _Fiscal deficit is important in exams._

---

### 🟡 **Capital Expenditure (CapEx)**

- Money spent on infrastructure, roads, housing, cities, etc.
- Union Budget allocates **big funds (₹11 lakh+ crore)** for infrastructure.

---

### 🟡 **Tax & Revenue**

- Govt collects money from taxes:
  - **Direct taxes** (Income tax)
  - **Indirect taxes** (GST, customs)

- Net tax receipts grow year-on-year.

## 📉 5️⃣ RBI & Macroeconomics

### 🔹 **Monetary Policy**

- RBI adjusts **repo rate** to control inflation and support growth.
- Recent moves lowered the repo rate to boost liquidity.
