## 1️⃣ Constitution Basics 📜

* The **Constitution of India** is the **supreme law** of India.
* **Adopted:** 26th Nov 1949 | **Came into force:** 26th Jan 1950
* **Length:** One of the longest constitutions in the world
* **Parts, Schedules, Articles:** Structure to organize rules

---

## 2️⃣ President of India 🇮🇳

* **Term:** 5 years
* **Election:** Indirect, by an Electoral College
* **Eligibility:**

  * Citizen of India
  * 35+ years old
  * Qualified for Lok Sabha elections
* **Example:** Droupadi Murmu (current President)

---

## 3️⃣ Fundamental Rights ✊

* **Total:** 6 Fundamental Rights (originally 7)
* **Examples:**

  * Right to Equality ⚖️
  * Right to Freedom 🗽
  * Right against Exploitation ❌
  * Right to Freedom of Religion ⛪🕌🕉️
  * Cultural & Educational Rights 📚
  * Right to Constitutional Remedies 📝
* **Remember:** Rights ensure **freedom + equality + justice**

---

## 4️⃣ Schedules of Constitution 📑

* **Schedules:** 12 (originally 8)
* **Purpose:** Organize government rules
* **Example:**

  * 1st Schedule: States & Union Territories
  * 9th Schedule: Laws protected from judicial review

---

## 5️⃣ Education-related Bodies 🏫

* **CBSE:** Central Board of Secondary Education
* **NCERT:** National Council of Educational Research & Training
* **UGC:** University Grants Commission
* **NTA:** National Testing Agency
* **Example:** CBSE conducts **Class 10 & 12 board exams**

---

## 6️⃣ Polity Basics 🏛️

* **Parliament:** Bicameral

  * **Lok Sabha:** House of the People (Lower House)
  * **Rajya Sabha:** Council of States (Upper House)
* **Constitutional Articles:**

  * **Article 14:** Equality before law
  * **Article 19:** Freedom of speech & expression
  * **Article 21:** Right to life & personal liberty

## 8️⃣ Economy Basics 💰

* **GDP (Gross Domestic Product):** Total value of goods & services produced in a country
* **Inflation:** Rise in general price levels over time
* **Other Key Terms:**

  * Fiscal Policy: Government spending & taxation
  * RBI: Controls money supply & interest rates
* **Example:** If rice costs ₹100 last year & ₹105 now → 5% inflation
