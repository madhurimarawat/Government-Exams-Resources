# 📘 The Companies Act, 2013 – Important Chapters Explained

This document explains **key chapters of the Companies Act, 2013**: 

---

## 🏢 Chapter III – Prospectus and Allotment of Securities

### 📌 What is this chapter about?
This chapter governs how a company **invites the public to subscribe to securities** and how those securities are **allotted**.

### 📖 Official Definition (Simplified)
A **prospectus** is *any document that invites the public to subscribe to or purchase securities of a company*.

📌 *(Section 2(70), Companies Act, 2013)*

### 🧾 Key Points
- Prospectus must contain **true, fair, and complete disclosure** 📄
- Covers:
  - Red herring prospectus
  - Shelf prospectus
- Allotment must follow **legal procedure**
- Severe punishment for **misstatement**

### ⚠️ Important Exam Points
- Civil liability + Criminal liability
- Company, directors, promoters can be punished
- Investor protection is the core objective 🛡️

### 🧪 Example
👉 If profit is shown as ₹10 crore instead of actual ₹2 crore, it is a **misleading prospectus** → punishable.

## 💰 Chapter IV – Share Capital and Debentures

### 📌 What is this chapter about?
Explains **structure of capital**, issue of shares, and debentures.

### 📖 Official Meaning (Simplified)
- **Share Capital** = money raised by issuing shares
- **Debenture** = written acknowledgement of debt

### 🧾 Key Points
- Types of share capital:
  - Equity share capital
  - Preference share capital
- Bonus shares allowed 🎁
- Buy-back of shares (subject to conditions)
- Debentures may be **secured or unsecured**

### ⚠️ Important Exam Points
- Debenture holders are **creditors**, not owners
- Shares = ownership, Debentures = loan
- Company cannot issue shares at discount (except sweat equity)

### 🧪 Example
👉 Equity shareholders vote in AGM; debenture holders do not.

## 👥 Chapter VIII – Declaration of Dividend

### 📌 What is this chapter about?
Regulates **payment of dividends** to shareholders.

### 📖 Official Meaning (Simplified)
**Dividend** is the portion of profit distributed to shareholders.

### 🧾 Key Points
- Dividend declared only out of:
  - Current profits
  - Past reserves
- Payment within **30 days**
- Unpaid dividend → **Unpaid Dividend Account**

### ⚠️ Important Exam Points
- No dividend if company has losses
- Dividend cannot be paid out of capital
- Protection of shareholder interest

### 🧪 Example
👉 If dividend is not claimed for 7 years → transferred to IEPF.

---

## 🧑‍💼 Chapter X – Audit and Auditors

### 📌 What is this chapter about?
Deals with **appointment, powers, duties, and removal of auditors**.

### 📖 Official Meaning (Simplified)
An **auditor** examines company accounts to ensure **true and fair view**.

### 🧾 Key Points
- Auditor appointed in AGM
- Mandatory auditor rotation for certain companies
- Auditor has right to:
  - Access books
  - Seek explanations

### ⚠️ Important Exam Points
- Auditor is independent
- Auditor reports to shareholders, not directors
- Heavy penalties for fraud

### 🧪 Example
👉 Auditor reports manipulation in accounts → legal action follows.

---

## 🧾 Chapter XI – Appointment and Qualifications of Directors

### 📌 What is this chapter about?
Defines **eligibility, appointment, and disqualification** of directors.

### 📖 Official Meaning (Simplified)
A **director** is a person appointed to manage company affairs.

### 🧾 Key Points
- DIN (Director Identification Number) mandatory 🆔
- Minimum directors:
  - Private: 2
  - Public: 3
- Maximum directors: 15 (extendable)

### ⚠️ Important Exam Points
- Insolvent, convicted, or disqualified persons cannot be directors
- Director acts as **trustee and agent**

### 🧪 Example
👉 A convicted fraudster cannot become director.

---

## 🧠 Chapter XII – Meetings of Board and its Powers

### 📌 What is this chapter about?
Regulates **board meetings** and **decision-making powers**.

### 📖 Official Meaning (Simplified)
Board meetings are formal gatherings where directors exercise powers.

### 🧾 Key Points
- Minimum 4 board meetings per year 📅
- Quorum = 1/3rd or 2 directors (whichever higher)
- Powers include:
  - Borrowing money
  - Issuing shares

### ⚠️ Important Exam Points
- Collective decision-making is mandatory
- Certain powers cannot be delegated

### 🧪 Example
👉 Board approves merger proposal in meeting.

---

## ⚖️ Chapter XXVII – NCLT and NCLAT

### 📌 What is this chapter about?
Establishes **specialized tribunals** for company law disputes.

### 📖 Official Meaning (Simplified)
- **NCLT** → Original jurisdiction
- **NCLAT** → Appellate authority

### 🧾 Key Points
- Handles:
  - Oppression & mismanagement
  - Winding up
  - Mergers & amalgamations

### ⚠️ Important Exam Points
- Faster than civil courts
- Appeal from NCLAT → Supreme Court

### 🧪 Example
👉 Shareholders approach NCLT for mismanagement.
