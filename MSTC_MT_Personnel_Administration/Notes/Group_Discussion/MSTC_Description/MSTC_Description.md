# 🔹 What is “Mini Ratna” Status?

The Government of India grants **“Navratna” and “Mini Ratna” status** to financially strong PSUs to give them more autonomy in decision-making.

Mini Ratna PSUs are divided into:

## 🥇 Mini Ratna Category-I

To qualify:

* The PSU must have made profit for the last 3 consecutive years.
* Must have a positive net worth.
* Must meet certain financial performance benchmarks.

### Benefits:

* Can invest up to **₹500 crore or equal to its net worth (whichever is lower)** without needing government approval.
* Greater operational and financial autonomy.

👉 MSTC is a **Mini Ratna Category-I**, which means it is financially stable and has higher autonomy compared to Category-II PSUs.

---

## 🥈 Mini Ratna Category-II

To qualify:

* Must have made profit for the last 3 consecutive years.
* Positive net worth required.
* But financial thresholds are lower than Category-I.

### Benefits:

* Can invest up to **₹300 crore or up to 50% of net worth (whichever is lower)** without prior government approval.

---

# 🔹 Difference Between Category-I and Category-II

| Feature            | Category-I           | Category-II              |
| ------------------ | -------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Financial Strength | Higher               | Moderate                 |
| Investment Limit   | ₹500 Cr or Net Worth | ₹300 Cr or 50% Net Worth |
| Autonomy           | More                 | Less                     |

So being **Mini Ratna Category-I** means MSTC:
✔ Is financially strong
✔ Is consistently profitable
✔ Has greater operational independence
✔ Has government trust and stability

# 🏛 What Are Maharatna, Navratna & Mini Ratna?

The Government of India grants these statuses to high-performing **Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs)** to give them **greater financial and operational autonomy**.

These classifications are given by the **Department of Public Enterprises (DPE)** under the Government of India.

They are basically **levels of autonomy**:

> 🥇 Maharatna (Highest)
> 🥈 Navratna
> 🥉 Mini Ratna Category-I
> 🏅 Mini Ratna Category-II

---

# 🥇 Maharatna (Highest Status)

This is the **top-level status** given to the biggest and strongest PSUs.

### Eligibility:

* Already a Navratna company
* Listed on Indian stock exchange
* Average annual turnover above ₹25,000 crore
* Net worth above ₹15,000 crore
* Net profit above ₹5,000 crore (over last 3 years)

### Benefits:

* Can invest up to **15% of net worth** in a project (up to ₹5,000 crore) without government approval.
* Can expand globally more freely.
* Very high operational freedom.

### Example:

* Oil and Natural Gas Corporation
* Steel Authority of India Limited

---

# 🥈 Navratna

Second-highest category.

### Eligibility:

* Must be a Mini Ratna first
* Must meet performance parameters set by DPE
* Strong financial and operational performance

### Benefits:

* Can invest up to **₹1,000 crore or 15% of net worth (whichever is lower)** without government approval.
* More freedom in joint ventures, subsidiaries, HR decisions.

### Example:

* Bharat Electronics Limited
* Container Corporation of India

---

# 🥉 Mini Ratna

Mini Ratna companies are profit-making PSUs that get moderate autonomy.

There are **two categories**:

---

## 🔹 Mini Ratna Category-I

### Eligibility:

* Profit in last 3 consecutive years
* Positive net worth
* Meets financial performance benchmarks

### Benefits:

* Can invest up to **₹500 crore or net worth (whichever is lower)** without government approval.

### Example:

* MSTC Limited

---

## 🔹 Mini Ratna Category-II

### Eligibility:

* Profit for last 3 consecutive years
* Positive net worth

### Benefits:

* Can invest up to **₹300 crore or 50% of net worth (whichever is lower)** without government approval.

Autonomy is less compared to Category-I.

---

| ------------------ | --------------- | --------- | ------------ | ------------- |
