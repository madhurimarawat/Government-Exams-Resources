# Overall review

So this was my first group discussion, and I was very scared and nervous about how it would go, but it actually went really nice. It went smoothly, and I learned a lot from it.

So the first thing is to have real-life examples. One thing that I felt is that we connect more to real life and real data instead of just theories. Not that theories or bookish answers are bad, but when it comes to a discussion, real-life examples and data should be given more importance. One more thing I felt is that your hand movements and body language matter a lot, sometimes even more than what you are saying. Some of the other candidates had very good points, but their body language was very closed or stiff, and it felt like they were just speaking to themselves. Also, one important thing I noticed is that people were mostly looking at the moderator instead of looking at the group, which is not something you should do in a group discussion.

Second thing is that I tried to interact with the group a lot. Whenever I was answering, I tried to involve others and take their opinions. I said things like yes or no, built on their points, and maintained eye contact with everyone. I listened carefully to every speaker, nodded my head, smiled at times, and tried to contribute positively. I also made sure to let others finish speaking. This is something I am really proud of and happy about, because I had planned beforehand that I would behave like this in the discussion, and it mostly went the same way. So this is something I would highly recommend to anyone going for a group discussion.

Follow these points: try to include real-life examples, maintain open body language, and actively engage with the group.

Another important thing is about language. When you are speaking, do not try to use very big or complex words just to impress. Try to use vocabulary that is clear and effective. It should be impressive but also simple and natural so that everyone can understand. It should not sound too bookish, because a discussion is not meant to sound like a textbook, it is meant to be interactive and natural.
